OPTION A — THE PHILOSOPHICAL ANCHOR (Read This First)

In 2001, Dr. Christopher Emdin stood in front of a classroom of Black and Brown scholars in the Bronx and announced he was their teacher. He had practiced his "teacher scowl" in the mirror. He believed success meant a quiet, contained classroom. He kept teaching through 9/11 sirens. He silenced a scholar who asked to call her mother. He ignored Islamophobic slurs hurled at Muslim scholars in his room because — he told himself — his job was to just keep teaching.

Twenty years later, he wrote: "Looking back now, I realize I was not actually teaching at all."

He calls what he learned Reality Pedagogy — reaching scholars where they really are, making sure their lives and backgrounds are reflected in the curriculum and classroom. It interrupts the idea that teaching is about managing behavior. Instead, scholars become co-teachers. The classroom becomes a space for dialogue — even when the schedule says there's no time. Even when the school says there's no space.

"The best teachers don't just keep teaching. Instead, they use their pedagogy as protest: They disrupt teaching norms that harm vulnerable scholars."

— Dr. Christopher Emdin, "Teaching Isn't About Managing Behavior"

OPTION D — THE FRAME FOR EVERYTHING YOU'LL SEE TODAY

Every video you watch today is a window into a teacher navigating a real classroom moment. As you watch, carry this question with you:

"Is this teacher managing scholars — or reaching them?"

The videos, the lenses, the Warrior Move — they all live inside that one question. W.A.I.T is how you slow down enough to reach. Circles is how you expand your reach. What you see on screen today is not a stranger. It's Emdin in 2001. It might be you last Tuesday.

1

W.A.I.T — What am I doing? What assumptions am I making? Is curiosity driving me? What does trying look like? This is how Reality Pedagogy begins.

2

Circles of Influence — Emdin says teachers place "metaphorical knees on the necks" of scholars. What is in your Circle of Control that you're using to manage instead of reach?

3

The ARMOR — Emdin's 9/11 story is a SHIELD failure. He had the tools to protect his scholars' humanity. He chose the scowl instead. What would SWORD look like in that moment?

OPTION E — WHERE THIS ALL LEADS (REALITY PEDAGOGY AS NORTH STAR)

After today's video analysis, the Warrior Move at the close of this session will ask you to name ONE thing you're committing to before practicum ends. That commitment is your first step into Reality Pedagogy. Here's how the tools you've built across JBC 1, 2, and 3 connect to it:

JBC TOOL HOW IT CONNECTS TO REALITY PEDAGOGY
W.A.I.T The pause before judgment is the space where co-teaching can begin. You can't hear scholars if you're already reacting.
Circles of Influence Reality Pedagogy lives in the Circle of Influence — you can't mandate it, you build it through relationship, dialogue, and trust.
Attention Signals Signals that honor scholar agency — not just compliance — are the entry points. How you get attention tells scholars who you think they are.
Sincere & Authentic Praise Emdin: scholars need a sense of agency. Praise that sees and names the real scholar — not the compliant one — builds that agency.
The Warrior Move Your Warrior Move today is your first act of Reality Pedagogy. Name the scholar. Name the moment. Name the move. That's where it starts.

THE BIG IDEA

You have been teaching for almost 3 weeks. You have tools. Now it's time to see them — or their absence — in action. Each group will watch a short video and apply the full toolkit you've built across JBC 1, JBC 2, and your Learning Environment sessions. Every lens connects. Nothing you've learned exists in isolation. And today, all of it lives inside one question from Dr. Christopher Emdin: "Is this teacher managing scholars — or reaching them?"

HOW IT WORKS
1

Find your group — Each group has a different video and a different primary lens.

2

3

Apply ALL the lenses — Your primary lens anchors the analysis. The connective lenses deepen it.

4

Report out — Each group shares their primary lens findings. Together you build the full picture.

STRUCTURE FOR EACH GROUP (3–4 min)

The connection: Which connective lens surprised you most — and why?

The transfer: Name ONE thing you're taking back to your classroom tomorrow.

Group 1 — Attention Signals
What attention signals were used — or completely absent? What was the cost of that absence?
How did the lack of a consistent signal connect to what happened with the teacher's engaging presence?
What signal would YOU use in that room — and what would you do differently from the jump?
Group 2 — W.A.I.T
What was the teacher NOTICING vs. what story were they telling themselves?
What assumptions drove the teacher's responses — and what did those assumptions cost the scholars?
What ONE question, asked with genuine curiosity, could have changed the trajectory?
Group 3 — Sincere & Authentic Praise
Where were the missed opportunities for sincere and authentic praise — even in a chaotic moment?
When the teacher DID acknowledge scholars, was it tied to actions or traits? Sincere or performative?
Write one example of sincere and authentic praise that would have worked in that specific moment.
Group 4 — Circles of Influence
Map what you saw: What was in the teacher's Circle of Control? What were they treating as Control that was actually Concern?
What was in the teacher's Circle of Influence that they never tapped?
Name one Ecosystem-level factor that might have been shaping what you saw — even if the video didn't show it.
⏱ Whole Group — 10 min
Whole Group Debrief

These questions pull across ALL four groups. The goal is to build one complete picture from four different lenses.

CONNECTION 1 — Everything Is Linked

Group 1 saw missing attention signals. Group 4 saw a teacher living in their Circle of Concern. How are these the SAME problem — just seen from different angles?

CONNECTION 2 — W.A.I.T Before Everything Else

Group 2 applied W.A.I.T to the teacher's assumptions. How does W.A.I.T show up — or fail to show up — in the moments Groups 1, 3, and 4 analyzed too?

CONNECTION 3 — The Praise That Wasn't There

Group 3 identified missed praise opportunities. How might ONE moment of sincere and authentic praise have changed the trajectory of what Groups 1, 2, and 4 analyzed?

CONNECTION 4 — The Honest Mirror

You've been teaching for 3 weeks. Without naming names — which moment in any of these videos felt closest to something you've experienced or done yourself?

CONNECTION 5 — The Transfer

What is ONE concrete move — grounded in any of the four lenses — that you are committing to try before Practicum ends?

🔄 RJ CONNECTION

In every video, there were scholars whose learning was interrupted. Restorative Justice asks: Who was harmed? What do they need? Who is responsible for repair? That's not just a question for the teacher in the video. It's a question for all of us, every day.

⏱ Individual Reflection — 5 min
Your Warrior Move

You didn't just watch those videos. You analyzed them through tools you've been building for 3 weeks. Now it's time to turn the lens on yourself — with the same curiosity you brought to the screen.

STEP 1 — NOTICE
Think about your classroom this week. What is ONE moment — messy, unresolved, or still sitting with you — that you are ready to look at differently?
Write it in your CM Workbook. Just the facts — no story yet.
STEP 2 — APPLY W.A.I.T
W — What did you actually notice? A — What assumptions did you make? I — What would you wonder if you led with curiosity? T — What does trying curiosity first look like for YOU in this moment?
STEP 3 — MAP YOUR CIRCLES
What is in your Circle of Control right now? What relationship in your Circle of Influence haven't you tapped yet? What are you treating as Concern that you could actually influence?
STEP 4 — NAME YOUR MOVE (YOUR FIRST ACT OF REALITY PEDAGOGY)
Based on what you saw in the videos AND what you just reflected on — what is the ONE move you are committing to before Practicum ends? Be specific. Name the scholar. Name the moment. Name the tool. This is not just a Warrior Move — this is your first step into Reality Pedagogy. Co-teaching. Dialogue. Reaching instead of managing.
This is your Warrior Move. Record it in your CM Workbook. We're holding you to it. ⚔️
STEP 5 — THE 3 A's
Close with one of the 3 A's. Aha: What shifted for you today? Appreciation: Who or what are you grateful for — specifically? Apology: What are you owning — and what is your forward commitment?
Remember — an apology without a commitment is just a statement. A warrior names the move.
